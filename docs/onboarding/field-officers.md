# Field Officer Onboarding Pack

This guide is for field officers and mobile-first inspection staff.

## First-Day Setup

- confirm mobile login works
- confirm the inspections tab loads
- confirm the case detail screen opens
- confirm camera/photo library/document permissions are working on the device
- review how sync indicators appear on mobile

## First-Week Learning Path

### Day 1

- read the [field officer SOP](/Users/michaeladeleye/Documents/product/ERP Surveyors/docs/sops/field-officers.md)
- review the inspection screen sections and submit readiness rules

### Day 2

- open a draft inspection from seeded data
- fill notes, summary, and structured fields
- save and confirm draft persistence

### Day 3

- upload inspection photos
- review pending photo and pending submit states
- practice manual sync actions

### Day 4

- upload a case-linked field document
- open an existing linked document from the case screen

### Day 5

- run one full inspection workflow:
  - open case
  - open inspection
  - complete fields
  - upload photos
  - submit successfully

## Role Readiness Checklist

The team lead should confirm the person can:

- navigate from case to inspection confidently
- complete inspection fields correctly
- use photos and documents correctly
- understand pending sync states
- avoid submitting incomplete inspections

## Daily Habits

- review assigned inspections early
- sync queued work when network is available
- check submit readiness before final submission
- escalate assignment or context issues immediately

## Weekly Habits

- review failed or delayed sync incidents
- review photo/document completeness on submitted inspections
- share recurring field blockers with operations

## KPIs To Watch

- inspection submission turnaround
- percentage of inspections submitted without rework
- average photo completeness on submitted inspections
- sync failure or retry frequency
- overdue assigned inspections

## 30-Day Signoff Questions

- can the person complete and submit inspections reliably?
- do they understand offline-safe behavior?
- are they capturing enough evidence for downstream review?
- can they handle document and photo flows confidently?
