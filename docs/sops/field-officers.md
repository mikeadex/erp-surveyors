# Field Officer SOP

This SOP is for field officers and mobile-first staff executing inspections, capturing evidence, and updating cases from the field.

## Primary Objective

Capture complete, reliable field evidence without losing work when network quality is poor.

## Main Areas You Own

- mobile inspection execution
- photo capture and upload
- field notes and summary quality
- case-linked document upload from the field

## Main Working Screen

Your primary tool is the mobile inspection workspace.

Use it to:

- open the assigned inspection
- fill occupancy and location details
- capture condition summary
- add inspector notes
- upload photos
- submit the inspection when complete

## Daily Workflow

1. Open the mobile app and review assigned inspections.
2. Prioritize draft or due inspections first.
3. Open the case if you need client/property context.
4. Complete the inspection carefully on site.
5. Upload photos as you go or when network returns.
6. Check sync indicators before leaving the work.
7. Submit only when all required information is complete.

## Inspection Quality Checklist

Before submission, confirm:

- inspection date is correct
- occupancy is filled
- location context is filled
- core condition fields are completed
- condition summary is clear
- inspector notes are meaningful where needed
- at least one photo exists

## Offline And Sync Rules

The mobile app now supports:

- local draft persistence
- queued draft retry sync
- queued photo upload retry sync
- queued submit retry sync

What this means operationally:

- if the network drops, keep working
- if a save fails, do not re-enter everything immediately
- use the visible sync actions when you are back online
- confirm that pending photos and pending submission have cleared before assuming work is fully synced

## Document Handling

From the case screen, you can:

- open linked documents
- upload additional case-linked supporting files
- remove case-linked documents where appropriate

Use this for field-acquired supporting records that should remain attached to the instruction.

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate when:

- the case or property context appears wrong
- the inspection cannot be completed because the assignment data is incomplete
- required evidence cannot be captured on site
- uploads remain stuck even after retry and reconnect

## Handoffs

- to valuers:
  submitted inspection with complete notes and photos
- to operations:
  any branch, assignment, or access issue discovered in the field
